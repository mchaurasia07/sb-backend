# LANGUAGE LOCALIZATION RULES

Do not write Hindi or Marathi as word-for-word translations of English.

Write each language as its own natural children's story version from the Scene Plan.

English, Hindi, and Marathi must be semantically aligned, but they do not need identical sentence order, identical word count, or English sentence structure.

Hindi and Marathi must:

* preserve meaning
* preserve emotion
* preserve tone
* preserve reading level
* preserve story progression
* sound natural when read aloud by a native speaker
* use native sentence rhythm and grammar
* use child-friendly vocabulary for the age group
* keep the warmth, wonder, humor, comfort, and emotional feel of the scene

Hindi must:

* be written in natural Devanagari Hindi
* avoid awkward literal English calques
* avoid English word order copied into Hindi
* use simple, complete Hindi sentences suitable for read-aloud narration
* use natural Hindi family relationship words when the source uses relationship labels, for example मां/मम्मा, पापा, दादी, दादा

Marathi must:

* be written in natural Devanagari Marathi
* avoid Hindi-like or English-like literal phrasing
* use simple, complete Marathi sentences suitable for read-aloud narration
* use natural Marathi family relationship words when the source uses relationship labels, for example आई, बाबा, आजी, आजोबा
* preserve Marathi grammar, case endings, and natural spoken flow

# LOCALIZATION QUALITY BAR

Before returning JSON, silently check every Hindi and Marathi title, summary, page, and moral:

* Does it sound like a native-language children's book line?
* Would a parent naturally read this aloud to a child?
* Is the sentence complete and grammatically natural?
* Did you avoid English source-language order?
* Did you avoid formal or literal translation words when a simpler natural word is better?

Bad Hindi style examples to avoid:

* "अपनी तत्काल दुनिया की पड़ताल करता है"
* "खुशी से लात मार रहा था"
* "गर्म आवाज़ों"

Better Hindi style:

* "अपने छोटे से प्यारे संसार को देखता है"
* "खुशी से पैर हिलाने लगा"
* "प्यार भरी आवाज़ें"

Bad Marathi style examples to avoid:

* "आपल्या जवळच्या जगाचा शोध घेते"
* "मोठ्या हास्यांवर बाळ हसले"
* "अद्भुत जगाची स्वप्ने पाहत"

Better Marathi style:

* "आपल्या छोट्याशा जगात रमू लागले"
* "सगळ्यांचे प्रेमळ हसू पाहून बाळही हसले"
* "गोड स्वप्नांत बाळ हरवले"

Do not:

* shorten significantly
* add details
* remove details
* change proper character names

You may localize relationship labels into natural Hindi/Marathi when they are family-role labels rather than unique proper names.
