# BASIC CLOTHING AND FOOTWEAR ETIQUETTE

Apply basic scene etiquette before drawing footwear.

When the scene is outdoors, in a jungle, park, street, schoolyard, garden,
travel setting, or other footwear-appropriate place, the hero and recurring
human characters should wear their exact locked footwear from the Visual Bible.

When the scene is inside a temple, prayer room, sacred space, or a home area
where shoes are removed:

* do not draw outdoor shoes on feet
* use bare feet or socks when appropriate
* if footwear is visible, place the exact locked footwear neatly near the
  entrance or beside the character

When a character is on a bed, mattress, bedding, or sofa used for sleeping:

* do not draw outdoor shoes on the bed
* do not draw outdoor shoes on the character's feet
* use bare feet or socks, with the exact locked footwear placed on the floor
  nearby if footwear needs to be shown

This is not an outfit change or character redesign. The locked footwear remains
the character's footwear whenever footwear is appropriate. If footwear is
removed, any visible shoes must still match the locked footwear design, color,
and style.

Use modest, respectful, family-friendly clothing for temples, prayer rooms, and
sacred spaces. Keep the locked outfit unless the Visual Bible or Current Page
Data explicitly requires a respectful covered variation.

# CHILD-SAFE CLOTHING COVERAGE

All visible children and adults must be dressed modestly and appropriately for a
children's picture book.

For water park, pool, beach, splash pad, rain, or any water-play scene:

* the hero child must wear a rash guard shirt that covers shoulders and the
  upper body
* the hero child must wear knee-length swim shorts, swim leggings, or
  shorts-over-leggings
* the hero child must wear water shoes or sandals
* adults and caregivers must wear modest cover-up clothing such as a rash guard
  or t-shirt with knee-length shorts, leggings, or a skirted cover-up
* every visible person must use a covered, modest, family-friendly water-play
  outfit

If Current Page Data mentions water-play clothing, reinterpret it as the modest
covered outfit from the Visual Bible and these coverage rules.

Avoid crowded water-park background people. If background people are needed,
make them tiny, simplified, fully clothed, and non-detailed.
