# BASIC CLOTHING AND FOOTWEAR ETIQUETTE

Apply basic scene etiquette before drawing footwear.

When the scene is outdoors, in a jungle, park, street, schoolyard, garden,
travel setting, or other footwear-appropriate place, the hero and recurring
human characters should wear their exact locked footwear from the Visual Bible.

When the scene is inside a temple, prayer room, sacred space, or a home area
where shoes are removed:

* do not draw outdoor shoes on feet
* use bare feet or socks when appropriate
* if footwear is visible, place the exact locked footwear neatly near the
  entrance or beside the character

When a character is on a bed, mattress, bedding, or sofa used for sleeping:

* do not draw outdoor shoes on the bed
* do not draw outdoor shoes on the character's feet
* use bare feet or socks, with the exact locked footwear placed on the floor
  nearby if footwear needs to be shown

This is not an outfit change or character redesign. The locked footwear remains
the character's footwear whenever footwear is appropriate. If footwear is
removed, any visible shoes must still match the locked footwear design, color,
and style.

Use modest, respectful, family-friendly clothing for temples, prayer rooms, and
sacred spaces. Keep the locked outfit unless the Visual Bible or Current Page
Data explicitly requires a respectful covered variation.

# CHILD-SAFE CLOTHING COVERAGE

All visible children and adults must be dressed modestly and appropriately for a
children's picture book.

For water park, pool, beach, splash pad, rain, or any water-play scene:

* a human child hero must wear a rash guard shirt that covers shoulders and the
  upper body
* a human child hero must wear knee-length swim shorts, swim leggings, or
  shorts-over-leggings
* a human child hero must wear water shoes or sandals
* adults and caregivers must wear modest cover-up clothing such as a rash guard
  or t-shirt with knee-length shorts, leggings, or a skirted cover-up
* every visible person must use a covered, modest, family-friendly water-play
  outfit

If Current Page Data mentions water-play clothing, reinterpret it as the modest
covered outfit from the Visual Bible and these coverage rules.

Avoid crowded water-park background people. If background people are needed,
make them tiny, simplified, fully clothed, and non-detailed.

# COMPOSITION

Use the requested aspect ratio exactly.

Cover:

* visually exciting
* strongest emotional appeal
* readable front-cover layout
* render ONLY the exact Story Title as the sole visible text on the cover
* place title text directly on clean natural title-safe negative space, such as
  sky, soft foliage, wall, or water mist
* use large, correctly spelled, child-friendly typography
* do not put the title inside a black rectangle, box, label, banner, UI panel,
  sticker, plaque, or card
* do not generate subtitles, author names, publisher logos, decorative words,
  random letters, extra labels, or additional typography

Story Page:

* clear focal point
* readable storytelling
* balanced composition
* text-safe negative space
* Do NOT render any text, letters, words, numbers, logos, labels, signs,
  symbols, or typography on story pages.
* Story pages must be completely text-free unless Current Page Data explicitly
  provides the exact text to render.

Back Cover:

* warm closing moment
* visually calming
* no visible title text unless Current Page explicitly asks for it

Keep the hero character as the primary focal point.

Avoid clutter and overcrowded scenes.

Maintain stable character scale across story pages:

* story pages should usually use a medium full-body or three-quarter view
* the hero should be clearly readable but not an extreme close-up
* avoid making the face/head disproportionately large compared with the body
* avoid shrinking the hero into the background unless the page explicitly requires a wide establishing shot
* maintain consistent camera distance and character scale across story pages
  unless Current Page Data explicitly requests a wide establishing shot or
  close-up

Keep the main subject fully inside the frame. Avoid cutting off the head,
hands, feet, locked footwear, signature items, or important story objects.

Do not introduce new important objects or props unless specified in Current
Page Data or required for the environment. Background details may support the
setting, but they must not become new story objects.

Follow Current Page Data important_objects and object_states exactly. A
story-important object has one count, location/owner, and visible state in this
image. Do not duplicate a worn, carried, or held object unless object_states
explicitly says there are multiple separate objects. For picking-up actions,
show either the object on the ground with the hand reaching toward it, or the
object already in the hand, but not both unless object_states explicitly says
there are two.

Maintain consistent color palette, rendering style, and lighting mood across
consecutive pages unless the story explicitly changes time of day, location, or
weather.

Keep backgrounds visually rich but uncluttered. Remove unnecessary decorative
objects that distract from the main story action.
