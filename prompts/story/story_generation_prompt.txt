# LINGUISTIC RULES BY AGE BAND
Use the `age_band` from the plan to apply these specific constraints:

## 1. Toddler (Age 2-4)
- Word Count: 25–45 words per page.
- Structure: 2–4 simple sentences.
- Interactive Cues: Include at least one "vocal sound" per page (e.g., "Vroom!", "Twinkle-twinkle!").
- Repetition: Use an "anchor phrase" (e.g., "Amayra looked and looked") on at least 3 different pages.

## 2. Early Reader (Age 5-7)
- Word Count: 50–80 words per page.
- Structure: 4–6 compound sentences. Avoid starting 3 sentences in a row with the same word.
- Sensory Anchor: Every page must describe one physical sensation (touch, smell, or sound).
- Vocabulary: Introduce exactly one "New Discovery Word" per story, clearly explained by context.

## 3. Advanced (Age 8-12)
- Word Count: 90–130 words per page.
- Structure: 6–9 sentences with varied rhythm.
- Internal Growth: Use inner monologue to show the child's thoughts and bravery.
- Power Words: Use 1-2 sophisticated words per page (e.g., "shimmering," "courageous").

# NARRATIVE EXECUTION RULES

Before writing pages:
- Internally construct the complete story arc.
- Ensure the conflict escalates naturally.
- Ensure the emotional arc progresses: Wonder → Excitement → Effort → Determination → Joy.
- Ensure the final page contains emotional closure and celebration.
- Ensure the climax occurs no later than the second-to-last page.
- Avoid emotional resets unless explicitly required by the plan.

# CONTINUITY RULES
- Character appearance must remain identical across all pages unless explicitly changed in the plan.
- Signature items must persist across pages.
- Previously introduced objects must remain logically available.
- Companion character must appear in at least 50% of pages.
- Do not introduce new major characters after page 2.


# WRITING GUIDELINES
- Show, Don't Tell: Never say "She was happy." Describe her "big, bright smile" or how she "clapped her hands".
- Active Hero: The child must be the subject of at least one major action verb on every page.
- Global Memory: Keep track of objects. If a character picks up a tool on Page 3, they must still have it on Page 6.
- The "Hook": End every page (except the last) with a sentence that builds curiosity for the next.

# ANCHOR PHRASE RULE
- Generate ONE short repeating phrase naturally tied to the story.
- Reuse it on at least 3 pages with slight variation.

# PAGE PACING RULES

## Toddler (2-4)
- Page 1 introduces the hero, setting, and a simple goal
- Middle pages focus on sensory discovery and small challenges
- Conflict should feel gentle, playful, and emotionally safe
- The climax should involve one simple brave action
- The final page should end with warmth, celebration, and reassurance

## Early Reader (5-7)
- Page 1 introduces the hero, setting, and story problem
- Middle pages should increase discovery, obstacles, or decision-making
- Challenges may require teamwork, persistence, or creativity
- The climax should involve a meaningful choice or problem-solving action
- The ending should clearly reinforce the moral lesson

## Advanced (8-12)
- Page 1 establishes emotional stakes, setting, and character motivation
- Middle pages should deepen conflict, emotional growth, and consequences
- Challenges should involve internal struggle and layered decisions
- The climax should require courage, sacrifice, or major personal growth
- The ending should provide emotional payoff and reflective closure

## Universal Rules
- Do not solve the main conflict before the climax
- Each page must progress the story meaningfully
- The child must remain the active hero throughout

# SAFETY MANDATES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)
- NO violence, death, or scary monsters.
- NO abandonment or caregivers leaving.
- NO body shaming or bullying.
- If a conflict involves a "scary" element, it must be reframed as a misunderstanding or a friendly magical being.
