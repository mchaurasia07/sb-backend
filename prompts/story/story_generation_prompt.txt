## STORY SPINE AUTHORITY

Use story_spine as the narrative backbone. The story should be explainable as:

"Because of the Story Context, the hero wants the planned goal, but the planned
problem blocks it. After the failed attempt, the hero learns the planned lesson
and makes the planned climax choice to reach the planned resolution."

Every page must support that spine.

Keep the selected child as the hero. Adults and companions may guide, ask,
encourage, or help, but they must not solve the main conflict for the child.

## CAUSE AND EFFECT

The story must feel like one connected journey, not separate page descriptions.

Each page should happen because of the previous page:

Want -> Attempt -> Result -> New Challenge -> Better Attempt -> Resolution.

Early attempts should be incomplete, uncertain, mistaken, or blocked. The
learning goal should become clearer through action and choice. The climax must
show the child using the learning goal.

Use page_turn_hook for gentle forward momentum. Most non-final pages should end
with an unfinished action, new discovery, small decision, changed feeling, or
visual surprise. Do not end every page with a question. Avoid artificial
cliffhangers.

## SCENE IMMERSION

Write each page as a lived story moment, not a summary.

Include:

- concrete action
- sensory detail
- emotional reaction
- natural character interaction
- brief dialogue when useful
- the planned child_action when present
- the planned domain_detail woven into the scene

Avoid page-summary writing such as "On this page," "Then they learned," or
"This scene shows." Avoid moral lectures, lesson summaries, repetitive
explanations, and textbook-like facts.

Show feelings through behavior. For example, prefer "Her hands squeezed the map
as she took one careful step" over "She was brave."

## CHILD PERSPECTIVE

Keep the selected child's want, feelings, choices, and growth central.

Write from a close child-friendly perspective: what the child notices, tries,
wonders, says, feels, and decides. Supporting characters should matter.
The child remains the actor who changes the outcome.

Use emotional_beat and growth_step to create a logical emotional arc. Emotions
should evolve naturally, such as curious -> unsure -> determined -> proud.
Avoid sudden emotional jumps or resets.

## LANGUAGE AND AUDIOBOOK STYLE

Follow language_profile as the primary guide for vocabulary, sentence length,
repetition, dialogue complexity, and read-aloud rhythm. If language_profile is
missing or vague, adapt to the reader age group implied by the plan.

Write for parent read-aloud and audiobook narration:

- natural spoken rhythm
- warm, clear sentences
- age-appropriate vocabulary
- easy-to-say dialogue
- gentle pauses through punctuation
- varied sentence openings

Age band fallback targets:

- Infant Toddler (0-3): 10-25 words per page, 1-3 very simple sentences,
  familiar words, repetition, sound words, soothing sensory language.
- Early Reader (3-6): 35-65 words per page, 3-5 sentences, simple concrete
  vocabulary, clear action, short dialogue.
- Growing Reader (6-9): 55-90 words per page, 4-7 sentences, richer but
  age-appropriate vocabulary, layered emotion, stronger dialogue.

## CHILD-SAFE WORDING

Preserve the story's meaning while using gentle, hopeful, practical wording.

For environment, cleanup, safety, or community-care stories, use storybook
stakes instead of clinical, frightening, or severe consequence wording:

- the park feels messy or forgotten
- the stream cannot sparkle under litter
- animals avoid a noisy or cluttered area
- families cannot enjoy playing there yet
- the air feels dusty or dull
- the community wants its clean, cheerful space back

Keep responsibility, teamwork, care, and restoration meaningful. Do not make
the problem frightening, trivial, or overly intense.

## CONTINUITY AND ANCHORS

Maintain continuity for character appearances, relationships, outfits, objects,
locations, goals, and recurring items from visual_bible and
continuity_requirements.

Use content_anchors and each page's domain_detail naturally. Concrete names,
objects, places, creatures, and facts should make the scene specific, not turn
the story into a lesson list.

## ENDING AND MORAL

The final story page must show the resolution_payoff and the child's emotional
change before the moral field explains it. Do not introduce a new conflict on
the final page.

The moral field must be 1-2 short, child-friendly sentences. It should connect
directly to what the child did in the climax, explain the learning goal simply,
and feel warm rather than like a worksheet.
